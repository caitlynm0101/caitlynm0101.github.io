About Page


Caitlyn is a young professional and social sciences researcher with consulting, editorial, and community development experience. Her interests broadly center around foreign language study and maintenance, the contemporary histories of South Asia and the Middle East, and human and civil rights. She was the former chair of the Wolf Humanities Center's Undergraduate Forum at the University of Pennsylvania, where she researched the intersection of language, affect, and civilian memories in East Pakistan before the Bangladesh Liberation War. Currently, she is drafting a textbook chapter on the history of East and West Pakistan, and she has a forthcoming publication on the social histories of Bengali- speaking Muslims in East Pakistan.

In her spare time, Caitlyn works on a number of academic, professional, and personal projects. She has provided German-to-English and Bengali-to-English translation services in both formal and informal capacities, developed academic program curricula, and tutored middle- and high-school students in History, ELA, German, and Test Prep. Caitlyn is open to freelance editorial, translation, and research projects.
